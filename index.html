Hi! I am a PhD student at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University (Virginia Tech). I am fortunate to work with my advisor, Prof. Guoqiang Yu. Before comming to Virginia Tech, I earned my master degrees from Nankai University and National TsingHua University through a joint master program, under the guidance of Prof. Jufeng Yang and Prof. Shang-Hong Lai. I was also lucky to have opportunities to work with Prof. Ming-Ming Cheng during my study at Nankai University.

My research lies on designing machine learning algorithms for decoding brain functions from the ever-increasing brain imaging data. Other than brain image analysis, my research interests span the areas of machine learning, optimization, applied statisitcs, and signal and image procssing.

I am graduating in 2021 and currently on the job market.

Ongoing Project

Fully Automatic Cell Tracking of Mouse Embryogenesis (10TB data) [Code]
Recent years have seen significant breakthroughs in microscopy techniques and fluorescence probes, which enables recording the mouse embryogenesis at cellular level for days â€“ from a single cell to the a fully formed organism - easily generating terabyte-level 3D time-lapse data. As millions of cells exist in the data, it brings a natural demand of an automated computational tool for its comprehensive analysis. At current stage, we are focusing on four key tasks: cell segmentation, cell tracking and data curation and visualization.
